History and Discovery

The Origin of a Revolution

The path toward X-ray diffraction began at the end of the 19th century and the beginning of the 20th. It was not a single discovery, but a chain of brilliance that allowed "seeing" for the first time the atomic structure of materials.

Fundamental Actors

  • 1895 - Wilhelm Röntgen: Discovers X-rays.
  • 1912 - Max von Laue: Confirmed the wave nature of X-rays and the atomic structure of crystals.
  • 1913 - The Braggs: Created Bragg's Law, allowing calculation of real atomic distances.

Fundamentals of Physics

The technique takes advantage of the fact that the wavelength of X-rays is comparable to the space between atoms in a crystal. The constructive interference of these waves, described by Bragg's law, allows obtaining information about the ordered arrangement of atoms.

Interdisciplinary Fundamentals

Biology and Chemistry

The Structure of Life: XRD was key to solving the mystery of DNA. By crystallizing biological molecules, the exact position of each atom is determined.

Phase Differentiation: Distinguishes polymorphisms (same composition, different structure) such as graphite vs diamond.

Interplanar Distance Formula

\( d_{hkl} = \frac{a}{\sqrt{h^2 + k^2 + l^2}} \)

Bragg's Law

\( n\lambda = 2d \sin(\theta) \)
  • n: Order of reflection.
  • λ: Wavelength.
  • d: Atomic distance.
  • θ: Angle of incidence.

Molecular Geometry

The laws of Sines and Cosines are used to calculate bond angles and distances in 3D space:

\( \frac{a}{\sin(A)} = \frac{b}{\sin(B)} \) | \( c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos(C) \)

Constructive Interference

Only when Bragg's Law is satisfied do the waves create a measurable intensity peak. Fun Fact: X-rays are used because their wavelength is so small that it can interact with the spacing between atoms.
